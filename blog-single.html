Mastering Goal Setting: A Science-Based Approach


  • Kagiri
  • Neurology
  • 14


You know as well as I do that setting and conquering goals is the heartbeat of self improvement. Drawing on the expertise of Dr. Andrew David Huberman, an esteemed neuroscientist, this short analysis explores the blend of neurobiology with psychology for an efficient goal-setting toolkit.

Understanding the Neural Circuits of Goals

  • Amygdala: Fear and anxiety association

  • Basal Ganglia: Action and inaction regulation

  • Lateral Prefrontal Cortex: Planning and long-term thinking

  • Orbital Prefrontal Cortex: Emotional orientation in goals


    • No matter how big or small your goal is, these brain circuits help figure how valuable the goal is and what actions you need to take to reach it.

    Best Practices in Goal Setting


    1. Priority Goal: Pioneering Your Progress

    First, a North Star goal is important. Set 1-3 goals per year for the best goal value. Also, identify the objective that aligns most closely with your ambitions. The 85% rule for optimal learning, states that you should choose a goal that you're 85% confident you can achieve. Goals need to be specific, measurable & tied to defined actions

    2. Aspiration and Ambition: The Catalysts of Achievement

    Don't shy away from audacious goals. If the goal is too easy: it doesnt recruit enough of the autonomic nervous system to pursue. Ambitious objectives stimulate creativity, and determination. Embrace a growth mindset while at it. Get specific about the verb & amount of time you will engage to achieve that goal – for example, get specific about how many miles per week you want to run and on what days

    3. Dont share your goals

    While the "Don't Tell the World" rule may be debated, it has been established that sharing goals before execution is detrimental to motivation. Even positive feedback can lead to premature validation without actual pursuit 🤫.

    4. Milestones and Metrics: The Pulse of Progress

    Break your goal into measurable components and set quarterly milestones. Then, set goals in 12-week or 3 -month intervals. This rhythmic approach allows for regular assessments and adjustments, ensuring you're consistently moving forward. Estimating how long it will take to achieve a goal completes this cognitive process.

    Dopamine

    Dopamine (Motivation) is highest when there is an exceptation of a positive and novel outcome. Crucially, self reward can have a counter effect of reducing motivation by bringing a sense of completion. Hence, use a randomised reward system to treat yourself after achieving a milestone. It can be as simple as a coin toss or a dice roll to ensure that the reward is not tied to the goal itself. Only reward yourself when the coin toss or dice roll is successful 🎲. Note: your phone can interfere with the reward system by providing a constant source of dopamine. - the best productivity app is no app 🔕

    5. The Power of Visualization: Envisioning Success

    Mentally rehearsing the achievement of your goal primes your mind for success 🧠. To do this, visualise the positive end state or the consequences of failure to build up motivation However, this is only useful at the begining of the goal. Focus-concentrate on your goal for 30 to 90 seconds before a workblock to use visualization as motivation and document with pen and paper.

    6. Time Chunking: Orchestrating Productivity

    Divide tasks into manageable segments. This technique cultivates focused attention, preventing overwhelm and ensuring steady progress toward your goal. When you encounter the middle problem, chunking can help you get back on track. Break tasks to shorter time segments and approach them with vigor.

    7. Aligning with Nature: Harmonizing Energy and Effort

    Consider your body's natural rhythms when planning your tasks. Synchronizing with your energy levels optimizes productivity, ensuring you're working at your peak. Peak motivation and attention times are 30 minutes, 3 hours, and 11 hours after waking up 🤗.

    So there you have it! Armed with Dr. Andrew Huberman's wisdom, you're set to conquer your goals. Remember, no goal is too small some goals are too small 🙃. So, take that first step! Happy goal-setting!
